About Us

Celebrating heritage, culture, and the spirit of ubuntu at the heart of Vilakazi Street.

Heritage & Sakhumzi’s Origin

In 2001, Sakhumzi formalised the shebeen into a restaurant. The house was converted, but the original doors, walls, and flooring were kept — preserving that authentic, lived-in feel that tells the story of Soweto’s resilience.

Years later, this old home is still buzzing. People come from across Mzansi and the world to experience true township flavour and ubuntu in one of Vilakazi Street’s most iconic spots.

And best of all? That deep-rooted tree is still standing strong — right where our story began.

Our Philosophy & Culture

At Sakhumzi, food is more than just a meal — it’s a celebration of community, togetherness, and heritage. Every dish we serve carries the warmth of home and the stories of our people.

We believe in ubuntu — “I am because we are.” From our kitchen team to our guests, everyone who walks through our doors becomes part of our family.

Whether you’re enjoying a traditional plate or simply soaking up the vibe, you’re experiencing a piece of Soweto’s living culture — served with love and soul.

Sakhumzi Vilakazi: Where it all began.

This is where it all began. Sakhumzi Vilakazi started in 2001, providing a home-away-from-home feeling. Where every customer is referred to as Champs, short for Champion. The restaurant is situated on Vilakazi Street, the heart of South African history. It is famous for its legendary African cuisine buffet, which is worth R270pp for 2 hours starting from 11:00- 21:00.

Sakhumzi Zoo Lake at Parkview, Joburg: Bringing the flavour to the city's green spaces.
